INNOVATION SPOTLIGHT — INTERNAL IDEA (HYPE #2026-01)
Breakthrough Technology for Hall 3

Selective Polymer-Retention: Dual-Acid Recycling for Mixed-Fiber Automotive Textiles

Originated by Jonas R.E. Glawion (Process Engineer, Autoneum Germany GmbH - Gundernhausen) — Submitted to HYPE Innovation Portal Feb 2026 — TRL 3-4 (lab-proven)

Problem: Mixed-Fiber Recycling Dead End

  • Flexi-Light PET, Propylat (PP+PET+cotton), Hybrid-Acoustics — cannot be sort-pure recycled
  • Mechanical shredding = "shoddy" with degraded polymer chains
  • Aggressive chemical recycling destroys both PET and cotton
  • Result: high-value materials lost to low-value downcycling
  • Zero recycling solution for mixed-fiber production waste + EOL returns

Solution: Dual-Acid Flash Separation

  • Specific dual-acid mixture heated to 216°C
  • PET matrix dissolves in 5 minutes (flash separation)
  • Cotton fibers remain 100% intact (structurally undamaged)
  • 97% pure PET monomer with full polymer chain integrity
  • Output: two pure streams — virgin-quality PET + intact cotton

Key Risks

  • Acid handling at >200°C requires closed-loop vapor recovery (EHS)
  • Solvent recovery rate must exceed 95% for viable OPEX
  • Binder fibers (bicomponent) and phenol resins may interfere
  • Process cost today higher than virgin PET — economics depend on CO2 taxes, ASR avoidance costs, and EU EPR regulations

Strategic Mitigations

  • Start with lab validation (<€50k) before committing to pilot
  • German research partners available (no US dependency)
  • Rising ASR taxes + CBAM + EU EPR will close the cost gap by 2028-2030
  • Phase 1+2 can run in parallel with mechanical recycling in Hall 3
  • USP: First supplier to guarantee "virgin-quality" recyclate from mixed-fiber automotive waste
