01 • Technology

HP Multi Jet Fusion — How It Works

Powder-bed fusion with voxel-level control via fusing and detailing agents

The MJF Process in 4 Steps

1
Powder Recoating — 80µm PA12 layer spread across build area
2
Agent Jetting — Fusing + detailing agents at 1200 dpi
3
IR Energy — Lamps fuse agent-treated areas; detail agent cools boundaries
4
Layer Repeat — Build progresses at ~3,466 cm³/hr (5600)

Key differentiator: Unlike SLS (single laser point), MJF fuses an entire layer simultaneously via infrared energy — enabling 10× higher throughput. The detailing agent provides sharp edges, smooth surfaces, and best-in-class isotropy without support structures.

Why €50–70/kg? — Powder Specification Drives Cost

MJF requires extremely fine powder with tight particle size distribution. This necessitates cryogenic grinding (not air-cooled pulverizing), which is the primary cost driver.

D50 ~56 µm
Median particle size (reference: PA2200)
D90 <84 µm
90th percentile (cryogenic required)
D10 ~35 µm
Fine fraction controls cohesion
45–90 µm
Optimal PSD range for MJF/SLS

Context for cost assessment: Standard PA12 pellet-to-powder transformation via air-cooled pulverizing (e.g., MAAG 85XLP) costs ~€0.03/kg but yields D90 ~300–500 µm — far too coarse for MJF. The D50 ~56 µm / D90 <84 µm specification requires cryogenic impact milling at –150°C (throughput ~50 kg/h), plus sieving, spheroidization, and quality control. HP’s RFID-locked closed ecosystem adds a proprietary margin on top. Raw PA12 pellets cost ~€3–5/kg; cryogenic grinding + classification adds ~€15–25/kg; HP’s certification + margin accounts for the rest.

Closed Material Ecosystem — No Third-Party Powder

  • All powders must be HP-certified with RFID-tagged cartridges — no third-party powder without voiding warranty
  • HP partners with BASF, Evonik, Arkema, Lubrizol for new material development
  • Effective cost per kg in build is much lower due to 80% reuse: PA12 effective = €10–14/kg (only 20% new powder per build)
  • Cost decomposition: PA12 pellets ~€3–5/kg → cryogenic grinding ~€15–25/kg → HP certification + RFID + margin → €50–70/kg retail
  • Future opportunity: if HP opens material ecosystem (competitive pressure from SLS), powder costs could drop to €25–35/kg

Safety Requirements (BetrSichV / ATEX / GefStoffV)

  • PA12 powder: Kst ~100–200 bar·m/s, MIE ~3–10 mJ — classified as combustible dust per EN 60079-10-2
  • ATEX Zone 22 (Category 3D equipment minimum) for all powder handling areas
  • All equipment, containers, operators must be grounded — ESD flooring + ESD wristband + ATEX-rated vacuum cleaners (Ruwac/Nilfisk)
  • PPE: FFP2 respirator (FFP3 for >30 min exposure), nitrile gloves (EN 374), dust-tight safety goggles (EN 166), ESD safety shoes (EN 20345)
  • Local exhaust ventilation (LEV): 2,000–4,000 m³/h per extraction point, HEPA H13/H14 filtration
  • Fire suppression: CO2 or dry powder extinguishers (no water on polymer powder); smoke/heat detection mandatory
  • Applicable: TRGS 720/722 (explosive dust prevention), BetrSichV, GefStoffV, DGUV Regel 109-001, ArbStättV
  • OEL: TRGS 900 respirable dust limit 1.25 mg/m³ (A-fraction) — particulate monitoring recommended

Post-Processing Decision: Do We Need Chemical Smoothing?

  • For our portfolio (jigs, fixtures, nozzles, brackets, tooling): NO — bead blasting is sufficient. Surface roughness Ra ~6–10 µm as-printed is acceptable for functional/internal parts
  • When chemical smoothing IS needed: Consumer-visible parts, fluid-tight housings, medical devices, parts requiring sealed surfaces
  • If we scale to inter-plant supply of customer-facing parts: DyeMansion VaporFuse (€80–120k) or AMT PostPro (€60–100k) can be fully automated — load batch, close door, automated cycle, no operator needed
  • Automation level: DyeMansion Powershot C (depowdering) = 10 min automated cycle. DyeMansion DM60 (dyeing) = automated batch. VaporFuse = fully enclosed automated chemical process. All can run unattended.
  • Phase 1 recommendation: HP Processing Station + €10–20k bead blaster. Add DyeMansion only when customer-facing or inter-plant volume justifies it.

  • Use case: AMR transports build units between printer → cooling station → depowdering → QC → point-of-use
  • Vendors: MiR250 (recommended, €58–83k deployed), OTTO 100, KUKA KMP, ABB AMR — market growing at 32.7% CAGR to $5.6B by 2026
  • Cost: MiR250 deployment €58–83k total (robot + charger + fleet software); payback 18–30 months — evaluate at Phase 2 once volume justifies
  • Integration: AMR fleet management connected to Apriso MES for automatic routing based on job status
