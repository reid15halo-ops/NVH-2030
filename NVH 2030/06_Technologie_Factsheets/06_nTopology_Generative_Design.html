Overview

What It Does

nTopology — Engineering Design for Additive Manufacturing

  • Implicit modeling platform: geometry defined by mathematical fields, not boundary meshes
  • Enables lattice structures, topology optimization, and functionally graded materials in a single workflow
  • Specifically designed for AM constraints: overhang angles, min feature sizes, build orientation
  • NVH relevance: Lattice-optimized acoustic structures can tune sound absorption properties when printed on MJF
  • Used by aerospace (Lockheed Martin), automotive (Scuderia AlphaTauri), and medical industries
  • Output: STEP, STL, 3MF — directly to HP SmartStream for MJF printing
How It Works

Process & Principle

Field-Driven Design

  • Step 1: Import design space (CAD model) + define load cases and constraints
  • Step 2: nTopology generates optimal topology/lattice within the design space
  • Step 3: Apply AM-specific constraints (MJF: min wall 0.5mm, min hole 0.5mm, no support needed)
  • Step 4: Functionally grade lattice density: dense where loads are high, open where sound absorption is needed
  • Step 5: Export production-ready geometry → nest in HP SmartStream → print
  • Parametric: change load case → entire lattice regenerates automatically
