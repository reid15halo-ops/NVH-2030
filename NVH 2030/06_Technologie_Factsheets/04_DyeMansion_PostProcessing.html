Overview

What It Does

DyeMansion — Print-to-Product Finishing

  • Complete automated post-processing ecosystem for powder-bed 3D printed parts (MJF, SLS, HSS)
  • Three core processes: Clean (depowdering) → Smooth (surface finish) → Color (dyeing)
  • All systems are fully enclosed, batch-automated — load parts, close door, press start, walk away
  • ISO-certified colors for end-use applications — automotive, medical, consumer products
  • Formally endorsed by HP as the recommended post-processing partner for MJF
  • Used by BMW, GM, Jabil, and all major MJF service bureaus globally
How It Works

Process & Principle

Three-Stage Finishing

  • Powershot C (Clean): Automated PolyShot Cleaning — proprietary media removes residual powder from part surfaces. 10 min per build-load. Replaces manual bead blasting.
  • Powershot S (Smooth): Automated shot peening / blasting for uniform surface texture. Consistent Ra across all parts in batch.
  • DM60 (Color): Automated dyeing — parts immersed in heated dye bath. ISO-certified black + custom colors. 2–4 hr cycle.
  • VaporFuse Surfacing: Chemical vapor smoothing — sealed, water-repellent surfaces without dimensional change. 2–6 hr automated cycle.
  • All processes work on MJF PA12, PA11, PP parts. TPU requires specific settings.

Relevance & Recommendation

Recommendation for Gundernhausen

  • Phase 1: HP Processing Station (included) + €15k bead blaster is sufficient for functional parts (jigs, fixtures)
  • Phase 2 (Month 7–12): Add Powershot C (€50–70k) when volume exceeds manual blasting capacity
  • Phase 3: Add DM60 dyeing + VaporFuse only if customer-facing or inter-plant parts require premium finish
  • For our portfolio (tooling, fixtures): Chemical smoothing is NOT needed — Ra ~6–10 µm as-printed is acceptable
  • When to invest: If Print Hub scales to consumer-visible parts for other plants, DyeMansion becomes essential
