Overview

What It Does

EV Acoustic PET Foam Sandwich — Autoneum's Strategic Future

  • PET foam core (ArmaFORM GR60/FR60) + needlepunch nonwoven face skins = lightweight acoustic sandwich
  • Dual function: sound insulator (blocks transmission) + absorber (dissipates energy) in one monomaterial part
  • 100% PET monomaterial → closed-loop recyclable, EU ELV regulation compliant from day one
  • 30–40% lighter than equivalent PUR-based parts — EV range optimization
  • Products: wheelhouse liners, underbody panels, door insulation, frunk liners, e-motor capsules, carpets
  • Target customers: BYD Hungary (300k vehicles/yr), Geely/Volvo, Leapmotor/Stellantis, Chery, NIO
How It Works

Process & Principle

Production Process

  • Step 1 — Fiber preparation: rPET fibers (up to 70% recycled PET bottles) fed to carding/cross-lapping
  • Step 2 — Needlepunch: ANDRITZ needlepunch line produces nonwoven face skins (30–4,200 gsm)
  • Step 3 — Foam core: In-house PET foam extrusion (twin-screw + CO2 blowing) OR bridge supply from Armacell ArmaFORM
  • Step 4 — Lamination: PET foam core bonded with nonwoven skins via thermoplastic ABC process (no latex, no water)
  • Step 4 — Thermoforming: Sandwich heated to 185–210°C, pressed into 3D shape. Close to zero spring-back after cooling.
  • Step 5 — Trimming: CNC router or waterjet trims to final contour
  • Step 6 — QC + Ship: Dimensional check, weight verification, VDA 4902 labeling, ship to OEM (e.g., BYD Szeged ~700 km)

  • EU ELV compliance: 100% rPET monomaterial = built-in recycled content certification from day one
