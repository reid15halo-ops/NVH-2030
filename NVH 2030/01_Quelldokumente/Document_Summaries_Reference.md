## 1. EN Flex Q1 2024 — Global Purchasing Meeting Summary

**Source:** `EN_Flex_2024_Q1_WT_Global_Purchasing_Meeting.pdf` (19 pages)
**Classification:** NDA — Do not share outside Autoneum

### Purpose
Summary of global purchasing meetings with three major PU chemical suppliers: **Huntsman**, **Dow**, and **Wanhua**.

### Key Supplier Updates

#### Huntsman — Sustainability Initiatives
- **Carbon footprint reduction** via biocircular benzene (+1500 EUR/ton premium), PCR PET trials (performance insufficient so far), and bio-based polyols (monopolistic dependency risk)
- **End-of-Life (EoL) foam recycling:** Advanced chemical recycling — Huntsman separates reclaimed liquid into 2 phases and reuses one phase directly. Need to understand customer acceptance of system expansion approach.
- **Mechanical recycling** (separate, sort, shred, compress): Tear strength is low, color inconsistent, trials ongoing. Solutions remain expensive.
- **High-temperature foam** (engine bay): Failed at 180°C/30min, works at 170°C/30min. Requirement is 180°C/5h — not yet met.
- **Heated floormats** using Huntsman's TPU elastomers portfolio (fully recyclable). Follow-up planned.
- **Theta-Cell S12:** Successfully switched at Chocen plant including new catalyst. Status pending for China/Oregon.
- **VE Foam systems:** Need internal homework on emission status of different foam systems and future RFQ requirements.

#### Dow — Sustainability Initiatives
- **Decarbonization:** Hydrogen plant, CO2 capture/storage, e-cracking at Terneuzen (NL). Target 40% reduction by 2030 → 25% CO2 footprint reduction on polyol. Products marketed as "DEC" (+500 EUR/ton).
- **Advanced chemical recycling:** Recycled/biobased feedstock via mass-balance. 60% circularity for iso, 75% for polyol. Marketed as "CIR" (+500 EUR/ton).
- **Renuva polyol:** From mattress recycling, up to 25% in slabstock polyol → 6% EoL recycling content.
- **VE Foam (SPECFLEX):** Commercial system at 60 kg/m³, demold 60-75s, passes BMW/Mercedes emissions. Ready for Mexico trial but price issue.
- **Theta-Cell:** Renuva polyol compatible. New FR being tested could lower graphite usage.
- **PUSH:** Propose SPECFLEX ACTIVE for faster demolding (gel + blow activity vs. blow-only in VORACTIVE).
- **Battery foam:** Proposing VoratronEP500 for battery filler foam.

#### Wanhua — Sustainability Initiatives
- **Bio-based polyether** (castor oil): 3 grades at 37%, 51%, 91% bio-content. Example: 15% bio-content acoustic foam. CO2 reduction 30-50% but at least 40% higher price.
- **Decarbonization:** Solar, wind, nuclear → >50% green electricity target.
- **CCUS platform** development ongoing.
- **Global supply:** 5 Chinese production sites, 1 EU MDI/TDI site. EU system house in 2025 (10kt flex foam, 50kt rigid). Mexico system house planned 2027 (Monterrey).
- **Low VOC:** Claims benchmark for aldehyde reduction in China.
- **Battery:** In direct contact with battery manufacturers.

### Relevance to NVH 2030 Layout
- Sustainability-driven material changes (bio-polyols, recycled content) may require different raw material storage and handling
- New foam systems (PUSH, VE, Theta-Cell) influence production equipment needs
- Supplier landscape evolution affects sourcing strategy for Gundernhausen

## 2. BIOPOL4MOTIVE — Bio-based PU Foam Project

**Source:** `20240924_BIOPOL4MOTIVE.pdf` (17 pages) + `Biopol4motive_Autoneum_CONFIDENTIAL.pptx` (22 slides)
**Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL — Do not share outside Autoneum (including JV partners)

### Project Overview
- **Consortium:** Oleon, Huntsman, Autoneum — managed by Flemish association "Catalisti"
- **Duration:** 33 months from April start, extended 6 months (to end of June 2024) after re-scoping with VLAIO
- **Target:** Create two grades of flexible PU foam (HR and VEF) with **35-40% bio-content** for automotive acoustic applications
- **Goal:** Achieve 25% sustainable content on total carpet part level

### Technical Approach
- Two-step process (pre-polymer method)
- Bio-polyol development for both A-side (isocyanate pre-polymer) and B-side (polyol blend)
- Funnel approach to down-select to 3 final bio-polyols:
  - 1 bio-polyol for A-side (shared by both grades)
  - 1 bio-polyol for B-side VEF grade
  - 1 bio-polyol for B-side HR grade
- Bio-polyol requirements: polyether-polyester based, non-food-competing, REACh registered

### Carbon Footprint Results
- Baseline carpet GWP: **34.1 kgCO2eq** (total carpet weight 11.5 kg → 2.96 kgCO2eq/kg)
- With Huntsman bio-formulation:
  - Excluding biogenic carbon: **-3.4% to -3.9%** reduction
  - Including biogenic carbon: **-5.4% to -6.9%** reduction

### Production Chain (Reference Product)
- Tufted carpet + backing: **Heckmondwicke (UK)**
- Heavylayer: **Gundernhausen (DE)**
- Backfoaming + assembly: **Bor (CZ)**
- Part weights: Front 7.5 kg, Back 4.0 kg | Total material used: 15.7 kg | Waste: 4.2 kg

### Trial Results (Winterthur, KW37)
- VEF bio foam (Acoustiflex VEB 29970) and HR bio foam (Acoustiflex HRB 30510) tested
- **Tear strength appeared low** for both VEF and HR
- Young's modulus: Autoneum measurements consistently higher than Huntsman (up to 40% difference)
- Loss factor: Mixed results, ~20% differences
- Root cause of discrepancies: Different test methodologies (load mass, sample count, interpolation methods, test sequences)
- Follow-up: Exchange samples, align measurement protocols

### Relevance to NVH 2030 Layout
- **Gundernhausen directly involved** — produces heavylayer for the reference carpet
- Bio-based foam requires compatible raw material handling infrastructure
- New formulations may need adapted mixing/dosing equipment
- Space needed for bio-polyol storage (different from conventional polyols)

## 3. Bloom Foam Project — Foam Reduction Bloomsburg

**Source:** `20242001_Foam EN_Bloom Foam Project.pdf` (12 pages)

### Problem Statement
- Bloomsburg (NA) plant has **high overpack (25-50%)** on thin-geometry parts
- Foam rises too quickly → tool closes onto foam → collapses → energy lost to fill tool
- Example: BT1YL Wheelhouse — nominal 180g, actual 315g (75% overpack)

### Solutions Investigated

#### 1. Delayed Catalyst
- Huntsman tried modifying current 31950 formula to slow initial rise
- **Result:** Unable to finalize a working formula → no trial conducted

#### 2. Low Density Visco (LDV)
- Huntsman RG50 visco foam formula tested
- Better at filling thin geometries on flat samples
- Trial results showed foam weight savings (108-219g per part)
- **However:**
  - Price increase ($3.09/kg → $3.31/kg) heavily offset weight savings
  - Cure time increase by **+60 seconds** killed all potential savings
  - Annual savings of ~$148k before cure time impact
  - **Project potentially cancelled** due to unfavorable economics

#### 3. Aiken Visco 32085
- Proven more capable at thin geometries
- Nominal density 70 kg/m³ (vs. 50 kg/m³ target)
- Customer currently receives 60-100 kg/m³ anyway — could work if customer accepts RG70

#### CapEx Requirements
- **Bulk tank mixer needed** — Bloomsburg doesn't have one. Options:
  - New mixing blade: ~$100k
  - Pulsair bubble mixer: ~$12,650 (4 bubblers + control unit)

### Relevance to NVH 2030 Layout
- Demonstrates foam process optimization challenges relevant to any new layout
- Thin-geometry filling issues may apply to Gundernhausen production
- Equipment decisions (mixers, dosing) inform layout planning
- Shows importance of matching foam system to tool geometry in layout planning

## 4. CO2-based Release Agents — Ellzee Plant Technology

**Source:** `Developements and posibilities in using release agents at flexible foam.pdf` (14 pages)
**Author:** Erich Jeckle, PU Foam Development, Ellzee plant

### Technology Overview
- Transition from aqueous (water-based) to **CO2-based release agents** (RA)
- CO2 replaces water as the carrier matrix for RA components
- Result: More concentrated RA, significantly reduced VOC emissions
- RA diluted with liquid CO2 via mixing system, transported via **high-pressure pipes** to production units
- Applied via standard spray guns as a white mist

### Infrastructure Required
- **Liquid-gas CO2 tank**
- **Cooling compressor / deep-freeze unit**
- **Mixing units** (RA concentrate + liquid CO2)
- **High-pressure lines and distributor valves** throughout manufacturing halls
- **Thermally insulated distributor fittings**
- Connection to foam carousels, single mould carriers, RA robots, spray pistols

### Performance Benefits (Ellzee Experience)
- **RA consumption reduced 60-70%** (up to 80% with next-gen mixing units)
- **Cleaning cycles extended** by 0.5-1.5 cycles/week less cleaning
- **Cycle time shortened** due to reduced venting time
- **Reduced scrap** from RA overuse
- Further 10-20% savings possible with new RA formulations
- Technology transferable from flexible foam to rigid foam (potential 50% cost savings)

### Cost Development at Ellzee (2015-2022)
- Progressive switch from aqueous to CO2-based RA:
  - 2015: 44,711 kg aqueous + 12,300 kg CO2
  - 2022: 4,420 kg aqueous + 11,250 kg CO2
- Adjusted for price increases (20-22% cumulative 2017-2022): **~35,000 EUR savings realized**
- Total RA costs 2022: ~113,500 EUR (down from ~225,800 EUR in 2015, partly volume-driven)

### Consequences of Reverting to Aqueous RA
- At least 1 year conversion period (former aqueous RA no longer produced)
- Risk of **+3 tons/year VOC emissions**
- RA consumption increase of **3-4x**
- Increased rejects and reduced quality for complex parts
- Cycle time extensions (more liquid to evaporate)
- Tool cleaning increase of **50-100%**
- Additional downtime: 45-55 min/tool/cycle for primer start-up

### Running Cost Comparison (Ellzee)
- CO2-based annual cost: ~97,000 EUR
- If reverted to aqueous: ~137,000 EUR (RA only) + 40,000-60,000 EUR additional cleaning
- **Net additional cost if reverting: 75,000-95,000 EUR/year** + secondary costs

### Relevance to NVH 2030 Layout
- **Critical for Gundernhausen layout:** CO2 RA infrastructure requires specific space allocation
  - CO2 tank placement
  - High-pressure piping routes
  - Mixing unit locations
  - Compressor/cooling units
- Trennmittel (release agent) handling area was flagged as critical in the questionnaire
- Technology transfer to Gundernhausen would require similar infrastructure investment
- Layout must accommodate RA distribution system to all foam production positions

## 5. PUSH HR Genk Trial #2 Summary

**Source:** `PUSH HR for Genk Trial 2 summary for EN.pdf` (11 pages)
**Date:** 22-23 April 2025 | **Presented:** 13 May 2025 | **Author:** Wolfgang Tenbrock

### Trial Outcome
- **Trial #2 was unsuccessful** — no good parts produced
- Root cause: Iso and polyol pumping setup not optimal; most trial time spent flushing the system

### Key Issues
- Foam remained unstable even after 3rd flushing
- Potential root causes identified:
  1. Flushing insufficient
  2. Isocyanate may have expired
  3. Bad mixing of polyol prior to 1st flushing or before 3rd loading

### Diagnostic Testing
- **Pre-flushing polyol test (from IBC):** Foam cell structure looked OK; slightly less rise height vs. Winterthur reference (likely due to 20°C vs 25°C raw material temperature)
- **During-trial polyol test (from day tank):** Lower rise height, very slight instability signs
- **Handmix tool comparison (40x40x2 cm, 50g/L target):**
  - Reference polyol + reference iso: Center OK, voidy in corners
  - Day tank polyol + reference iso: Voidy in entire part
  - Day tank polyol + day tank iso: Voidy in entire part
  - Reference polyol + day tank iso: Center OK, voidy in corners
- **Conclusion:** Polyol from the day tank caused instability — either insufficient flushing or insufficient IBC mixing

### Flushing Analysis
- Method: Drain → load 100L → circulate 30 min over 4 mixing heads → drain → repeat 3x
- Problem: This only dilutes material in the ring line. With 100L in ring line, after 3rd flush still **12.5% old material** remained. With 200L fills: **4.2% contamination**.
- Action items: Determine ring line volume, fix 3-way valve, add second drain pipe

### Next Steps
- Further handmix cross-checks of Genk samples
- Prepare better flushing procedure
- Order new polyol blend and fresh isocyanate
- Check HMI improvements for dosing unit

### Relevance to NVH 2030 Layout
- PUSH HR is a key foam technology being evaluated for production
- Demonstrates importance of **ring line design, flushing infrastructure, and day tank configuration** in layout planning
- Dosing unit HMI and piping layout directly impact production cell design
- Lessons learned on raw material handling infrastructure (IBC storage, mixing, circulation)

### Elwis-S Testing (BMW/Mercedes Standard)
- Transmissibility measurement using porous sample + known mass + shaker
- Transmissibility function interpolated around peak frequency (±25 Hz)
- Interpolant methods differ: 1-DOF system vs. polynomial — this is a key source of measurement discrepancies between Autoneum and Huntsman

### Foam Trials WK50 Planning
- OLEON formulation trials on flat and 3D tools
- Huntsman formulation on 3D tool (VEF only, 2 density × 2 ratios)
- Constraint: 90 kg needed to flush system, minimum 20 kg in system for production → 70 kg available for trials
- Time constraint: Trials cannot extend past Tuesday 19th (pre-year-end cleaning)

## 8. Cross-Document Insights for NVH 2030 Layout

### Infrastructure Requirements Identified

| System | Space/Infrastructure Need | Source Document |
|--------|--------------------------|-----------------|
| CO2-based RA | CO2 tank, compressor, high-pressure piping, mixing units | Release Agents (Ellzee) |
| Bio-polyol storage | Separate storage for bio-based raw materials (castor oil-based, etc.) | Biopol4motive, Purchasing Meeting |
| Foam dosing/mixing | Day tanks, ring lines, mixing heads, flushing infrastructure | PUSH HR Genk Trial |
| Foam carousel | Central production area with multiple mould positions | Release Agents, Layout proposals |
| Ventilation/extraction | Low-emission foam systems still require extraction; reduced with CO2 RA | BGEU EN Flex, Release Agents |
| Quality testing | Elwis-S equipment, acoustic/mechanical testing capability | Biopol4motive |
| Raw material handling | IBC storage, mixing/circulation equipment, temperature control | PUSH HR Genk, Bloom Foam |

### Technology Trends Affecting Layout Design

1. **Shift to bio-based materials** — New raw material types require adapted storage and handling
2. **CO2-based release agents** — Significant infrastructure investment but major operational savings
3. **PUSH foam technology** — Faster demolding (SPECFLEX ACTIVE) impacts cycle time and line capacity
4. **Sustainability requirements** — Circularity/recycling content targets may add processing steps
5. **Multi-supplier strategy** — Huntsman, Dow, Wanhua all developing competing sustainable solutions; layout should be flexible enough to accommodate different chemistries

### Key Decisions for Gundernhausen Layout

1. **Will CO2 RA technology be implemented?** (Drives significant infrastructure in Trennmittel area)
2. **Which foam systems will run at Gundernhausen?** (PUSH HR, VEF, bio-based, or all?)
3. **What is the target product mix post-2030?** (NVH decreasing, but which new products fill the gap?)
4. **Will bio-polyol processing be on-site or sourced externally?**
5. **How many foam carousel positions are needed in the consolidated Hall 2 layout?**
